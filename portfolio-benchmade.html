Benchmade Knife Co.

Product Launch Campaign

Manufacturing Craft.

Benchmade Knife Company has been selling premium knives since 1986. With recent years of rapid growth and a strong reputation of a quality product, one of their strongest differentiators is collaborating with international custom knife makers. From these collaborative knife designs, Benchmade builds a near idenitical replica at their factory in partnership with the designer. This further distinguishes the brand and helps the Benchmade brand spread its customer base. These designers all have a unique way of building their product, but they all share a love for their craft and following deep-rooted traditions.

This year, Benchmade was introducing two collaborations from two very different designers. The Saibu, a gentlemans knife designed by a traditional Japanese craftsman, and the Mini Pocket Rocket, a Shane Sibert classic, a perfect representation of the 'strong mans' large, bulky, and heavy work knife. While these products differed tremendously, both products were launching simultaneously, so Benchmade wanted to launch to market with a unified campaign featuring both products. This campaign, besides standard launch assets like an introductory email blast and web banners, they both needed to be featured at an upcoming Tradeshow. Benchmades blog was also launching at the time, and these designers stories would be the first content.

The simple yet difficult process of hand-crafting; the meituclous focus on details and slow progress of building these custom knives was the primary focus for the campaign. While each designer had photographs supplied from their repsective workshops, we captured close up photography of the Benchmade counterparts being built in the factory. Matching these custom knife makers' processes with Benchmades' emphasized the attention to detail and quality behind their product.

The meld between processes, showing benchmades process against that of our custom knife collaborators seamlessly joined the craft shown by both parties. Benchmade had a great showing at Bladeshow, the knife industries largest tradeshow. The Benchmade Blog launched successfully, leading with stories from each of the collaborating designers.
