Portland Center Stage

Event Collateral

Secluded Mystery

The Armory is a theatre located in Portland, Oregon’s Pearl District. Almost exclusively hosting live performances, the theatre has a strong following for fans of story telling and live acting. Each year, the location hosts an average of 13 different plays, typically spanning over a month at a time.

This year, a proposed collaboration with This American Life would bring several new plays to Portland Center Stages line up, each with unique artwork that related to the story. This artwork would be used on a wide variety of collateral pieces, so modularity is an important requirement. “The Middle of Nowhere” is a story about Nauru, an extremely small island in the Atlantic Ocean, that after seeing human colonization in the 1800’s, became a center point for a variety of unfortunate, illegal and unethical activities.

To be most effective at far away distances, complementary color was used to catch a viewers attention. Initially based off of a hand sketch, the drawing was scanned and redrawn in vector format. A strong feeling of isolation and discomfort with extra weight on the left side of the poster all add to the story of Nauru's scarred history. Supporting outdoor banner graphics were created in unison with a large format poster, which the artwork could easily be transferred to other print and digital uses.

The final design both showcased the dramatic isolation of Nauru, while not portraying any one of it's historical impacts. This is left as a mystery for the viewer, you'd never expect such a beautiful island to have such a dark history, and while we hint at it, that surprise was amazingly well received from spectators and fans during the story.
